Microsoft 365 Copilot

Your AI assistant woven into the Microsoft 365 apps you use every day — grounded in your work data through the Microsoft Graph.

Word · Excel · PowerPointOutlook · TeamsCopilot ChatAgents

1 What is Microsoft 365 Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot combines large language models (LLMs) with your organization's data and the Microsoft 365 apps. You give it a natural-language prompt and it drafts, summarizes, and analyzes — grounded in your emails, documents, chats, and meetings through the Microsoft Graph.

LLM + your data

Reasons over your work content, not just its training data.

Inside the apps

Lives in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams.

Enterprise-grade

Your data stays in your tenant and inherits your existing permissions.
The one idea to rememberCopilot doesn't just generate text — it grounds every prompt in content you already have access to, then reasons over it. Grounding is what makes answers specific to your work.

2 How Copilot works (the orchestration loop)

Every prompt passes through an orchestration process before and after the model:

  1. You type a prompt in an app or Copilot Chat.
  2. Copilot grounds it using Microsoft Graph + the semantic index — retrieving the emails, files, and chats you're allowed to see.
  3. It builds a modified prompt and sends it to the LLM.
  4. The model returns a response; Copilot post-processes it (compliance checks, citations, responsible-AI checks).
  5. The grounded, cited answer appears in the app.
Permission-aware by designCopilot only surfaces content the signed-in user already has access to. It never bypasses your Microsoft 365 permissions.

5 Writing effective prompts

A strong prompt usually has four parts: a goal, useful context, a source to ground on, and the expectations for the output.

Weak:  Summarize this.

Strong: Summarize the attached proposal in 5 bullet points for an
        exec audience. Flag risks and list the 3 decisions we need.
PatternExample prompt
Summarize"Summarize the last 10 emails from Maria about the Contoso deal."
Draft"Draft a friendly reply accepting the meeting and proposing Thursday."
Analyze"In this workbook, which region grew fastest last quarter and why?"
Create"Build a 6-slide deck from this Word doc, one slide per section."
Iterate, don't restartTreat it as a conversation: refine with "make it shorter", "more formal", or "add a table" instead of rewriting the whole prompt.

7 Extending Copilot with agents

Agents connect Copilot to specific knowledge, data, and actions. They are packaged and governed like other Microsoft 365 apps.

TypeWhat it isBuilt with
Declarative agentCopilot's model + your instructions, knowledge, and actionsAgent builder, Copilot Studio
Custom engine agentYour own model/orchestration surfaced in CopilotTeams Toolkit, pro-code SDK
  • Discover and add agents from the Agent Store.
  • Ground them on SharePoint, Dataverse, or line-of-business systems.
  • Build no-code/low-code in Copilot Studio or the in-app agent builder.
  • Manage them centrally in the Microsoft 365 admin center.

8 Admin & readiness

Most of the work to get a tenant ready is data hygiene and governance, so answers are high-quality and appropriately scoped.

LeverWhy it matters
SharePoint Advanced ManagementReduce oversharing and clean inactive sites so sources are trustworthy.
Restricted SharePoint SearchScope what Copilot can ground on while you tidy permissions.
Sensitivity labels (Purview)Classification flows into Copilot answers and exports.
Microsoft 365 admin centerLicense assignment and central agent management.
Oversharing is the #1 riskCopilot makes existing access visible fast. Audit broad-permission sites and "anyone" links before rollout, not after.

9 Get started in four steps

  1. Assign Microsoft 365 Copilot licenses in the admin center.
  2. Run a data-readiness pass (SharePoint Advanced Management + label review).
  3. Pilot with a focused group; collect prompts that work for each role.
  4. Roll out with enablement: a prompt library and 2–3 high-value scenarios per team.
