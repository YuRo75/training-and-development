1 What is Microsoft 365 Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot combines the power of large language models (LLMs) with your organization's data and the Microsoft 365 apps. It takes natural-language prompts to draft content, summarize, and analyze information — all grounded in your emails, documents, chats, and meetings via the Microsoft Graph.

LLM + your data

The model reasons over your work content, not just its training data.

Inside the apps

Available directly in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams.

Enterprise-grade

Your data stays in your tenant and inherits your existing permissions.
Grounding is the key ideaCopilot doesn't just generate text — it retrieves relevant content you already have access to, then uses the LLM to reason over it. That grounding is what makes answers specific to your work.

2 How Copilot works

Every prompt flows through an orchestration process before and after the model:

  1. You enter a prompt in an app or in Copilot Chat.
  2. Copilot grounds the prompt using the Microsoft Graph — retrieving the emails, files, and chats you have permission to see.
  3. The grounded prompt is sent to the LLM, which reasons and generates a response.
  4. Copilot post-processes the response (compliance, citations) and returns it in the app.
Permission-aware by designCopilot can only surface content the signed-in user already has access to. It never bypasses your existing Microsoft 365 permissions.

One prompt box, many agentsFrom Copilot Chat you can call agents — specialized assistants that bring extra skills and knowledge into the conversation.

5 Extending Copilot with agents

Agents customize and extend Copilot by connecting it to specific knowledge, data sources, and actions. They are packaged and managed just like other Microsoft 365 apps.

TypeWhat it isBuilt with
Declarative agentCopilot's model + your instructions, knowledge, and actionsAgent builder, Copilot Studio
Custom engine agentYour own model/orchestration, surfaced in CopilotPro-code (Teams Toolkit, SDK)
  • Discover and add agents from the Agent Store.
  • Connect agents to SharePoint, Dataverse, or line-of-business systems.
  • Govern them centrally in the Microsoft 365 admin center.

6 Admin & readiness

Getting a tenant ready is mostly about data hygiene and governance so Copilot returns high-quality, appropriately-scoped answers.

LeverWhy it matters
SharePoint Advanced ManagementReduce oversharing and clean up inactive sites so Copilot's sources are trustworthy.
Restricted SharePoint SearchScope what Copilot can ground on while you tidy permissions.
Sensitivity labels (Purview)Classification flows into Copilot responses and exports.
Microsoft 365 admin centerLicense assignment and central agent management.
Oversharing is the #1 riskCopilot makes existing access visible fast. Audit broad-permission sites and links before rollout, not after.

7 Get started

  1. Assign Microsoft 365 Copilot licenses in the admin center.
  2. Run a data-readiness pass (SharePoint Advanced Management, label review).
  3. Pilot with a focused group; gather prompts that work for your roles.
  4. Roll out with enablement: a prompt library and a few high-value scenarios per team.
