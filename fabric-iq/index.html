1 What is Fabric IQ?

Fabric IQ is the semantic intelligence layer of Microsoft IQ. It provides context on your business entities and data — modelling ontologies, semantic models, graphs, and data agents so AI can reason over analytics in OneLake and Power BI.

Builds on FabricFabric IQ is a workload in Microsoft Fabric. It unifies business semantics across data, models, and systems to power intelligent agents and decisions.

2 Ontology: modelling your business

The heart of Fabric IQ is the ontology — an item where you define the entity types of your business and how they relate. It turns raw tables into meaningful business concepts an agent can understand.

Entity types

Business concepts — Customer, Order, Product — not just tables.

Relationships

How entities connect, so agents can traverse meaning.

Semantics

Shared definitions of measures and terms across systems.
Why semantics matterAn agent that knows "revenue" and how a Customer links to Orders gives consistent, trustworthy answers — instead of re-deriving logic per query.

4 Data agents

Data agents let business users discover and chat with governed Fabric data sources in natural language. As of June 2026, Fabric Data Agents are generally available inside Microsoft 365 Copilot, with admin-managed publishing and Entra ID-based access.

From data to the flow of workUsers ask questions of governed data right where they already work — in Microsoft 365 Copilot — without leaving for a separate analytics tool.

5 Ontology rules & Activator

Fabric IQ integrates with Fabric Activator through Ontology Rules: define conditions and trigger actions on business entities using business language — turning insight into automated action.

  1. Model your entities in the ontology.
  2. Define rules in business terms (e.g. "flag any Account whose risk crosses a threshold").
  3. Activator monitors the entities and fires actions (alerts, flows) when conditions are met.
